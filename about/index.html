think/fish/shark tank

It is a THINK TANK (imagining new possibilities), FISH TANK (fishing for dates), and SHARK TANK (like the TV show), because many of our crushes are yet to be invented.

Existing representations of sex robots, AIs, and virtual / augmented reality companions largely reinforce the desire of colonial hard-ons and cis-hetero-patriarchal dollars… Human-Computer Love asks you to speculate on what futures might feed your own needs and desires.

artist's statement

This project was conceived in 2018, amidst a flurry of sensationalist press related to the nascent market for sex robots. Human-Computer Love aims to cultivate a space for imagining alternate futures of what technologies of intimacy might look like, besides those being produced by mainstream media and capitalist forces. It is collaborative inquiry into how these technologies might make sex more accessible, queer desire, decolonize beauty standards, and cultivate liberatory models for intimacy and care.

In regards to the personal-ads format, 2018 also marked the passing of FOSTA-SESTA into US federal law. These bills made websites legally responsible for the acts solicited on their listings, delivering a heavy blow to online free speech and creating a template for censorship across the web. Craigslist took down their personals section, Reddit removed multiple Subreddits, and Backpage.com + many other personals sites shut down. The impact on the sex worker community was huge — if not devastating — for many people who used these platforms to safely solicit and screen clients.

In general, online platforms for stating, seeking, and finding desire took on renewed significance as threatened spaces under the shadow of FOSTA-SESTA and a market saturated by dating apps with quick-swiping picture-profiles.

Humancomputerlove.com is an homage to these sites, a love letter in itself.

In this sense, the website straddles both technologies of the past and those of the future. It is a nostalgic nod to the internet of yesteryears, and also a speculative visioning for future pathways to intimacy.
